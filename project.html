Step-by-Step Working Principle

1

Power Input

230V AC main supply is fed into the system

2

Voltage Step-Down

Step-down transformer (220V to 12V) reduces voltage for safety and component protection

3

Current Sensing

Shunt resistor (0.01Ω) creates proportional voltage drop based on current flow (Ohm's Law: V = I × R)

4

Signal Amplification

LM358 op-amp amplifies tiny voltage differences and compares input vs output current

5

Switching Action

2N2222 transistor acts as a switch to activate higher-power alert devices

6

Relay Control

12V SPDT relay provides electrical isolation and controls high-voltage systems

7

Protection

1N4007 flyback diode absorbs voltage spikes to protect circuit components

8

Alert System

LED indicators and buzzer provide immediate visual and audio alerts when theft is detected

Component Details

Shunt Resistor

0.01Ω, 5W

Acts as current sensing element. Low resistance prevents circuit disruption while producing measurable voltage proportional to current.

LM358 Op-Amp

Dual Op-Amp IC

The brain of the sensing section. Amplifies micro-voltage drops and works as a comparator to detect current mismatch.

Transformer

220V-12V Step-Down

Provides safe operating voltage with electrical isolation to protect sensitive components from high voltage.

2N2222 Transistor

NPN Transistor

Converts low-level op-amp signals to high-current signals capable of driving relay and buzzer.

Relay

12V SPDT Coil

Electromagnetic switch allowing low-power circuits to control high-power systems with electrical isolation.

Flyback Diode

1N4007

Ensures protection by absorbing high-voltage spikes caused by relay coil switching.

LED Indicator

With Current Limiting Resistor

Provides immediate visual feedback when theft is detected. Resistor prevents LED burnout.

Buzzer

12V Active Buzzer

Provides audio alert for immediate notification when theft occurs, essential for practical deployment.

Project Summary

The Power Theft Detector provides a practical, low-cost, and efficient solution to one of the biggest challenges faced by power suppliers. Using a simple principle—comparing input and output current through shunt resistors—we've created a system that can detect illegal power tapping in real time.

With components like LM358, 2N2222, relays, LEDs, buzzers, and transformers, the project blends analog electronics with measurable results. The design is robust, expandable, and safe. It can be upgraded with GSM, IoT, or microcontrollers for advanced monitoring.

This project is not only technically strong but also socially impactful, helping prevent financial losses and ensuring fair use of electricity for all consumers.
